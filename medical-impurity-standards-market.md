<p><p>Medical impurity standards differentiate based on the nature of impurities, leading to two primary market types: Organic Standards and Inorganic Standards. Organic Standards focus on impurities derived from carbon-based compounds, often found in herbal medicines and natural products, emphasizing the need for purity to ensure safety and efficacy. In contrast, Inorganic Standards pertain to non-carbon elements, such as metals or minerals, commonly associated with synthetic drugs. Both standards are crucial for regulatory compliance and maintaining product quality in the pharmaceutical industry.</p></p>
