<p><strong>The Corn-Based Ethanol Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Wet Milling Process Ethanol</li><li>Dry Milling Process Ethanol</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The corn-based ethanol market features two primary production methods: wet milling and dry milling. Wet milling involves soaking corn in water and using enzymes to separate components, resulting in not just ethanol but also valuable byproducts like corn oil and gluten. In contrast, dry milling is a simpler, more cost-effective process where whole corn kernels are ground, fermented, and distilled directly into ethanol. Both processes contribute to biofuel production, affecting pricing, market dynamics, and availability of co-products.</p></p>
