<p><p>The Acute Myeloid Leukemia (AML) treatment market features various regimens addressing the disease's complexity. The DC regimen combines donor lymphocyte infusion with chemotherapy, enhancing patient outcomes through tailored immunotherapy. The AVD regimen integrates cytarabine, idarubicin, and daunorubicin, providing a robust chemotherapy approach to target leukemia cells. In contrast, the VCD regimen employs a trio of vincristine, cyclophosphamide, and dexamethasone, focusing on reducing tumor burden. Each regimen aims to improve survival rates and enhance the quality of life for patients.</p></p>
