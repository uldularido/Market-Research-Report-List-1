<p><ul><li>Fluorescence In Situ Hybridization (FISH)</li><li>Chromogenic In Situ Hybridization</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The In Situ Hybridization market is primarily divided into two types: Fluorescence In Situ Hybridization (FISH) and Chromogenic In Situ Hybridization. FISH utilizes fluorescent probes that bind to specific DNA or RNA sequences, allowing for the visualization of genetic material in cells with high sensitivity and resolution. In contrast, Chromogenic In Situ Hybridization employs enzyme-linked probes that produce a colored reaction when exposed to specific substrates, providing a less sensitive but simpler visualization method. Both techniques are invaluable in research and clinical diagnostics.</p></p>
