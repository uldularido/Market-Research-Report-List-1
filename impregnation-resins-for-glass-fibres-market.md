Type I resins are typically used in applications requiring high thermal resistance, offering superior dielectric properties and chemical stability. They are commonly found in electrical insulation and aerospace components. Type II resins, on the other hand, are designed for applications that demand enhanced mechanical strength and durability. These resins often find applications in automotive and construction industries where structural integrity is a priority. Each type serves distinct industry needs.</p></p>
